The M.A.M System for Arabic Transliteration A Standardized 1:1 QWERTY Phonetic Romanization Framework for Modern Standard Arabic Author: M.A.M Date: June 2026 License: Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0) ________________________________________ Abstract Traditional Arabic transliteration standards (e.g., ALA-LC, DIN 31635) fail in digital automation due to two systemic flaws: multi-character digraphs (e.g., sh, dh) which break 1:1 character parsing, and the omission/inconsistent mapping of short vowels, which destroys morphological recovery. Conversely, informal Arabizi utilizing alphanumeric substitutions (3, 7, 9) lacks academic consistency and ignores grammatical inflections. The M.A.M system solves these issues by establishing an absolute, unyielding 1:1 bijective ratio between the Arabic script (orthography/morpho-phonology) and a highly standardized Latin extended alphabet. By repurposing archaic, obsolete, and extended Latin characters (þ, ð, ſ, đ, ƭ, ƶ, ħ, ȝ, ś) alongside a strict vowel-mapping matrix and an innovative overring (˚) for Shaddah (gemination), M.A.M eliminates ambiguity. This makes it an ideal engine for lossless data compression, computational linguistics, machine translation, and rapid educational acquisition.________________________________________ 1. System Core Principles The M.A.M System operates under three rigid structural parameters: 1. Zero Digraphs: No two Latin characters may combine to represent a single Arabic phoneme. Every consonant is structurally independent. 2. Absolute Acoustic Transparency: Hidden short vowels (Fatha, Damma, Kasra) and final grammatical case inflections (I'rab) must be explicitly written at all times. [6, 7, 8] 3. Pure Acoustic Mapping: The system completely deletes silent calligraphy elements (such as connecting Alifs) and forces the explicit spelling of phonetic sun-letter assimilations. ________________________________________ 2. The Over-Circle Gemination (Shaddah) Rule ( ̊ ) Traditional systems double the consonant or introduce complex cursive glyphs to represent the Arabic Shaddah. The M.A.M System implements a highly streamlined, modern typographic overlay: a small, un-filled circle ( ̊ ) centered directly over the consonant. Capitalization Protocols for the M.A.M System To maximize clarity, proper nouns and sentence heads prioritize structural pronunciation over typography. If a word begins with an archaic character, the uppercase equivalent is used at the sentence head. For system consistency, lowercase remains acceptable in pure digital coding data strings to avoid altering the glyph geometry.
